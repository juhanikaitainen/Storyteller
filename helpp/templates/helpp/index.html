1. What is this website about?

1.1 Overview

This is a website for selling, buying and reading interactive stories. Storyteller is a project made by a college student for evaluation purposes (i.e. there is no real money involved in buying/selling the stories). However the stories shared on this plaform are real and everyone is free to sign up and read/write stories.

1.2 Interactive Stories

An interactive story is a story in which the reader can make choices about what happens next while reading the story, which not only makes it more immersive than a regular story, but also decides how the story progresses. This adds a whole new dimension to story-reading as different people arrive at different endings which makes up for an interesting discussion about how people make decisions in certain scenarios.

While there is certainly an opportunity for psychological study here, I (the Creator) have not explored it. This project is about the technical aspect of writing such stories and implementing the interactivity.

1.3 Usage Guide

You need to Sign Up for an account here to see any content whatsoever. A wallet is automatically created for you upon signing up which you can always access from the navigation bar on the top. It is good to add some money to your wallet before you do anything else. You can also see your transaction history from the same section.

Clicking on 'Stories' in the navigation bar takes you to the Story Home Page where you can explore and buy new stories or continue reading your old stories. Your reading positions are always saved.

1.4 Making Choices

When you 'read' a story, you are usually given a small paragraph (part of the story) along with one or more buttons. Clicking on one of these buttons decides how the story will move forward. If you have any issues or wish to provide feedback regarding this experience you are always welcome to mail me at storyteller.helpdesk@gmail.com.

1.5 Banned Users

You can get banned from the platform if you are found exploiting or misusing any functionality of this website. The "BAN" freezes your account so that you cannot access your wallet or stories. In case you think that you have been banned without a reason mail me at storyteller.helpdesk@gmail.com. There are multiple moderators on this platform and each one of them have the power to ban and un-ban users.

2. Information for Authors

2.1 The Art of Writing Interactive Stories

First of all you need a good story that has considerable number of choices. Here is an excerpt from the experience of one of our authors:

I sat down just after breakfast and wrote one story in broken sentences (the one which has a happy ending) and looked for places where I could branch out and create alternate narratives. Coming up with alternates is hard! With a bit of error and trial I figured that there needs to be a minimum of 5 choices along every possible path to make the story feel like an actual story. I also figured out that writing 25-1 = 31 different endings was impossible in 2 days. So I decided that some paths will have to merge.

I spent rest of the day coming up with 6-7 endings. This was the most entertaining task – It was a question of much did I want to get the protagonist beat up! Next morning I woke up early with a fresh mind and worked towards joining the half-completed plots with one of the endings. I ended up rejecting 2 endings that were “too gory” and “too edgy” compared to the rest. By the time sun had gone down, I was ready with a complete tree.

As evident from the authors' note, creating a story with several different endings is hard. Remember that every possible combination of choices must make a plausible story, otherwise the experience will be ruined for certain readers which defeats the whole purpose of 'making choices'.

2.2 Become a Writer

To write stories you need to take permission from one of our 'moderators' by applying from your settings section. If you are currently logged into the website this link should take you to the right place.

After becoming a writer you'll note that you now have a 2-star badge on your profile and an option of "New Story" on your home page. Clicking on the "New Story" takes you to an online editor in which you can write and submit your stories.

2.3 The Science of Writing Interactive Stories

Writing an interative story is a much more involved process than writing a simple story. For starters, this website uses "JSON" format for interactive stories. If you don't know what JSON is or how to write JSON, given below are some useful learning resources:

  • JSON Video Tutorial
  • JSON Textual Material

These resources have more than enough JSON related material to get you started. Click here to learn the process of writing a story using JSON. After watching this video you will be literate enough to start writing your own stories. Remember that the website enforces strict rules on how you write the stories. Your stories will undergo screening from one of our moderators before being available for public access. Spamming or writing offensive stories can result in a "freeze" ban.

3. Information for Developers

  • DataBase - SQLite with ORM for Python
  • Backend - Django
  • Frontend - Bootstrapped HTML

4. Guidelines for Moderation

As a moderator, your actions can have major consequences on the experience of users on our platform. All pages on this website that are associated with a "MODERATOR" privilege have their titles in Golden Color with a warning "actions have consequences" to remind you of your position. Any misbehaviour on the part of a moderator can result in permanent ban from the platform. Given below are some rules that all moderators need to follow:

  1. Any user attempting to exploit the websites' functionality must be banned.
  2. Categorise a story into U/A group if it contains some blood violence (or light gore).
  3. Categorise a story into A group if it conatins profanity or dark humour.
  4. Do not approve a story if it contains personal information of anyone other than the author himself/herself.
  5. Frozen account shouldn't be un-frozen without communication with the moderator who froze it.
  6. While you are technically allowed to approve your own stories, your credibility will decrease if you do so often.
  7. Do not freeze other moderators yourself. Contact the Administrator (storyteller.helpdesk@gmail.com).
